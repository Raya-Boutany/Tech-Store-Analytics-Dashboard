# Tech Store Analytics Dashboard (SQL + Power BI)
## Overview

This project analyzes business performance for a tech store that sells products and offers repair services. The goal was to understand revenue sources, expense breakdowns, and overall profitability.

This dashboard helps answer key business questions:

1. Where is revenue coming from?
2. What expenses are impacting profit?
3. Which areas of the business are most profitable?

## Data Source

The sales data used in this project is based on the Superstore dataset sourced from Kaggle (in train.csv). This dataset includes detailed information on orders, products, customers, and revenue, and was used to simulate real-world retail sales performance.

Additional repair and expense data were created to model a more complete business environment.

## Tools Used
- SQL Server
- Power BI
- DAX
  
## Key Features

- Revenue breakdown between product sales and repair services
- Expense analysis by category (rent, utilities, salaries)
- Profit and profit margin tracking
- Sales performance by product category
- Repair profitability by device type

## Key Insights

- Product sales generate nearly all of the business revenue, while repair services contribute minimally
- Technology products drive the majority of total sales revenue
- Salaries and rent are the primary cost drivers
- Laptop repairs generate the highest profit among service offerings
- The business shows an unusually high profit margin due to relatively low recorded expenses
  
## Limitations

The profit margin observed in this analysis is unrealistically high, which highlights a limitation in the dataset. Expenses are not fully representative of real-world business costs.

As a result, this project focuses on demonstrating data modeling, dashboard design, and analytical insights rather than precise financial accuracy.
